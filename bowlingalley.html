BOWLING ALLEY

★1995★


This art work is at the Walker Art Center. It “marked [her] entry to the World Wide Web, connecting an actual bowling lane in downtown Minneapolis with a gallery at the Walker and a website.” It explores issues of power and access/ desire in public and private spaces. It is a cybernetic installation linking three public spaces-Walker Art Center's gallery 7, Bryant Lake Bowl, Minneapolis and the bowling website through ISDN lines and digital sensor data. “Bowling at Bryant Lake Bowl triggers changes in the chain of site connections, scrambling the gallery's laserdisc projection and interfering with netusers/cyberbowlers' paths through the website.” The cultural impact was changing the way people interacted with spaces and was created during the inception of internet art.
