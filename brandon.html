BRANDON

★1998-1999★


This work very much focuses on the life of Brandon Teena, a young transgender man. In 1993 he was raped and murdered in Nebraska when it was discovered that he was anatomically female. This project is a web project that uses the nature of the Internet as a means to explore Brandon’s tragic story. It used hyperlinked images of a disembodied human form, once-live chat rooms on the subject of crime and punishment, and graphic moving images in order to illuminate the wide-reaching effect of Brandon’s life and death. This was presented in the Guggenheim Museum and was its first official engagement with the then-emerging medium of Internet art and one of the first works of this medium commissioned by a major institution. The cultural impact was to spread information about visibility and issues facing the transgender community.
